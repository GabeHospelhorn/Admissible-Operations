# Why Optimization Fails in Non-Admissible Systems

Most organizations attempt to scale by improving existing workflows.

This often fails.

The issue is not execution.

It is that some workflows should not exist at all.

---

## 1. The Optimization Illusion

Teams identify inefficiencies and attempt to fix them:

- improve process
- add automation
- increase staffing

Short-term improvement occurs.

But the underlying structure remains unchanged.

---

## 2. Rework as a Signal

Rework is not a minor inefficiency.

It is a structural signal.

If a workflow consistently requires:
- correction
- clarification
- intervention

It is not stable.

---

## 3. Human Correction as a Dependency

When workflows depend on human judgment to complete:

- variability increases
- throughput decreases
- scaling becomes expensive

This creates hidden coupling between people and system function.

---

## 4. Scaling Behavior

At low volume:
- systems appear functional
- humans absorb variability

At high volume:
- correction load exceeds capacity
- delays increase
- coordination cost explodes

---

## 5. Why Optimization Makes It Worse

Optimizing a non-admissible workflow:

- increases throughput of flawed output
- amplifies downstream correction
- hides structural failure behind metrics

---

## 6. Example — Revenue Cycle

RCM teams:

- correct documentation errors
- chase missing information
- resolve inconsistencies

This creates the appearance of a functioning system.

In reality:
RCM is compensating for upstream instability.

---

## 7. Example — Call Centers

Call centers scale to handle:

- confusion
- scheduling errors
- system mismatches

They do not solve the problem.

They absorb it.

---

## 8. Removal vs Optimization

Two approaches:

---

### Optimize

- improve speed
- reduce cost locally
- maintain structure

Outcome:
fragility remains

---

### Remove / Redesign

- eliminate unstable workflow
- restructure inputs and outputs
- reduce dependency on correction

Outcome:
stability improves

---

## 9. The Selection Problem

Scaling requires deciding:

Which workflows are allowed to exist?

Not:
How do we improve everything?

---

## 10. Practical Approach

1. Identify workflows with rework
2. Trace source of instability
3. Redesign or remove workflow
4. Only then optimize

---

## Closing

Systems fail when they preserve what should be removed.

Selection precedes optimization.

Without it, scale amplifies failure.
